<h3>Defining Scope and Objectives</h3>

- **Client Objectives:**
    
    - Essential for a successful engagement.
        
    - Discussed between the client and the red team to create mutual understanding and clear expectations.
        
    - Set the basis for engagement documentation and planning.
        
    - Define the tone and structure for the rest of the engagement.
        
- **Types of Engagements:**
    
    - General Internal/Network Penetration Test: Less focused, uses standard Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs).
        
    - Focused Adversary Emulation: Emulates a specific Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) or group, tailored to the company's industry.
        
- **Scope:**
    
    - Varies by organization and defines what is and isn't allowed during the engagement.
        
    - Typically defined by the client.
        
    - Red team can discuss grievances if the scope affects the engagement.
        
    - Example verbiage includes restrictions on data exfiltration, production servers, specific IP ranges, and system downtime.


<h3>Rules of Engagement</h3>

Rules of Engagement (RoE) are a legally binding outline of the client objectives and scope with further details of engagement expectations between both parties.

<h3>Campaign Planning</h3>

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|**Type of Plan**|**Explanation of Plan**|**Plan Contents**|
|Engagement Plan|An overarching description of technical requirements of the red team.|CONOPS, Resource and Personnel Requirements, Timelines|
|Operations Plan|An expansion of the Engagement Plan. Goes further into specifics of each detail.|Operators, Known Information, Responsibilities, etc.|
|Mission Plan|The exact commands to run and execution time of the engagement.|Commands to run, Time Objectives, Responsible Operator, etc.|
|Remediation Plan|Defines how the engagement will proceed after the campaign is finished.|Report, Remediation consultation, etc.|
